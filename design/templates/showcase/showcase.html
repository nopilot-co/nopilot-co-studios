The idea in one move · scroll right for detail

We make a business legible — to itself, and to the agents acting on its behalf.

Most AI pilots stall for one reason: the business cannot see itself. Its intelligence — strategic intent, operating reality, proprietary knowledge — is scattered across heads, documents and systems. We capture it, structure it, and make it readable. We call the result a Context Operating System. It is the substrate everything else stands on.

Drill in: how the lens works

Context has a shape. It runs from the executive layer — the schema, where intent lives — down to the enterprise — the data that fills the schema. Top-down framing without evidence is wishful thinking; bottom-up data without framing is noise. Built executive-first, enterprise-informed — and the business ends up owning it. Mapped along four dimensions: altitude, state, source, function.

The COS altitude lens — one legible map from the executive schema to the data.

Direct for control and margin. Channel for warm reach.
