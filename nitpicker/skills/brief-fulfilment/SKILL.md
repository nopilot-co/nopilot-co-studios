# brief-fulfilment

Judge the asset against the **brief it was meant to fulfil** — not against your
own taste. Score 1–5 for the `brief-fulfilment` dimension; this is a **gate**
(see review-policy), so a genuine miss fails the asset.

## Steps

1. **Read the brief** (`inputs/brief.md`) and the asset (the captured views from
   `visual-qa` and/or the source). If the brief is a stub, stop and get a real
   one — you cannot assess fulfilment without it.

2. **Extract the brief's demands** into a checklist:
   - **Objective** — the one thing the asset must achieve.
   - **Must-haves** — required content, sections, claims, assets, CTAs.
   - **Scope** — what's in and explicitly out.
   - **Constraints** — length, format, audience, deadline-driven specifics.

3. **Check the asset against each item.** For every demand, mark
   **met / partial / missing** with the evidence (where it is, or where it should
   be). Distinguish:
   - *Missing* — the brief asked, the asset doesn't deliver.
   - *Off-brief* — the asset does something the brief didn't ask for (scope creep
     or a wrong turn).
   - *Under-delivered* — present but too thin to do its job.

4. **Judge the whole.** Beyond the checklist: does the asset actually achieve the
   objective, or does it tick boxes while missing the point? A deck that includes
   every required section but doesn't make the case has fulfilled the letter, not
   the brief.

5. **Score and record.** Assign the 1–5 (5 = fully delivers the objective and
   every must-have; 1 = misses the objective or core must-haves). Write findings
   as concrete gaps with a suggested fix, into the review notes the `verdict`
   skill consolidates.

## Constraints

- Assess against the brief, not your preferences — note taste-level ideas
  separately as minor, not as fulfilment failures.
- Be specific: "missing the pricing section the brief required" beats "feels
  incomplete".
- If the brief itself is vague or contradictory, say so — that's a finding too.
