# audience-fit

Read the asset **as the target audience would** and judge whether it lands for
them. Score 1–5 for the `audience-fit` dimension.

## Steps

1. **Load the ICP** (`inputs/icp.md`) — who they are, their context, what they
   care about, their level of sophistication, their objections, and the
   solution/offering being put to them. If it's a stub, get a real profile first.

2. **Adopt their perspective** and read the asset against these lenses:

   **Linguistic** — is the vocabulary, register, and reading level right for
   them? Too jargon-heavy or too dumbed-down? Does it use *their* words for the
   problem, not the seller's internal terms?

   **Communication** — does it meet them where they are? Right amount of context
   (no assumed knowledge they lack, no over-explaining what they know). Right
   channel conventions and length for how this audience consumes things.

   **Content** — does it cover what *they* need to decide/act, in their priority
   order? Does it answer their actual questions and pre-empt their objections, or
   does it answer questions the seller wishes they were asking?

   **Solution fit** — is the solution framed around *their* problem and desired
   outcome, or around the product's features? Would they recognise their
   situation in it?

   **Offering fit** — is the offer (price, terms, ask, next step) appropriate and
   credible for this audience and this stage of the relationship? Is the CTA the
   right-sized next step for where they are?

3. **Find the mismatches.** Where would this audience disengage, disbelieve, get
   confused, or feel it's "not for me"? Be concrete about which line/section and
   which ICP trait it clashes with.

4. **Score and record.** Assign the 1–5 (5 = unmistakably written for this ICP and
   compelling to them; 1 = generic or aimed at the wrong reader). Write findings
   with suggested fixes into the review notes the `verdict` skill consolidates.

## Constraints

- Judge fit for *this* ICP, not a general audience. A piece can be excellent and
  still be wrong for the people it's aimed at — that's the finding.
- Separate audience-fit from tone-of-voice (the `tone-of-voice` skill owns voice
  mechanics) and from the so-what/yawn/sniff tests (the `apply-tests` skill).
  Overlap is fine; don't double-count the same point as the headline of two
  dimensions.
