# Template: outreach-email

> Reusable skeleton for a cold `outreach-email`. The `compose` skill fills each
> section in the brand's voice and replaces the bracketed guidance. Keep within
> the format ruleset (subject ≤ 60 chars, body ≤ 200 words, ≤ 2 links, one CTA).

```markdown
---
subject: "[≤60 chars — specific, no clickbait, references their world]"
preview: "[≤90 chars — the first line that shows in the inbox; complements, never repeats, the subject]"
channel: email
status: draft
---

<!-- hook -->
[One sentence that proves relevance: a trigger, a shared context, or a result
they'd recognise. Not "I hope this finds you well."]

<!-- body -->
[2–3 sentences. What you noticed → what you do about it → why it matters to them.
Concrete and specific to them; no feature lists.]

<!-- cta -->
[One low-friction ask. A question or a 15-minute suggestion — not "let me know
your thoughts". A single link at most.]
```

## Notes

- Subject and preview do different jobs — don't restate one in the other.
- Exactly one CTA. If you're tempted to add a second, cut it.
- Lead with their situation, not your company.
