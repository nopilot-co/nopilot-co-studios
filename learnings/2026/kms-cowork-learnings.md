Saved the durable learnings to memory. Here is the summary.

## What we built
A Coherence-branded proposal to **Indicia** (the lead agency) to partner on rebuilding the **Konica Minolta Sensing Americas** website. It exists as an interactive showcase-HTML and a 25-page branded landscape PDF, backed by a rate-card-costed commercial model, all tracked through the studios engagement at `assets/documents/strategy-consulting/`.

## The approach, in phases
1. **Shaped through the studios cast.** Principal to producer to the design, commercial, and writer roles, with every job and decision logged in `engagement.json` and the ledger.
2. **Brand, done properly.** Ingested Coherence into the design-studio, then corrected it to the authoritative Brand Guidelines v1.2: raspberry #EC145A, Poppins, the COHERENCE wordmark, slate neutrals.
3. **Content as a wraparound.** Mirrored Indicia's own scope-to-launch structure and added the piece they were missing: a Phase 0 stakeholder-engagement layer. Built out context (category shift, headless web, performance measurement), a decision-blockers section, a cover, a swimlane timeline, case studies, and the commercials.
4. **Voice rewritten to house style.** Plain, consultative British English. No dashes, no slogans, no inflated phrasing.
5. **Commercials from the rate card.** A `deal.yml` of skills, days and rates, gated by the beancounter (45 per cent margin, passes), landing at about £267,000 across phases.
6. **Rendered to PDF.** Showcase HTML to PDF via Playwright honouring the print CSS, with native running header and footer (logo, confidentiality, company details, page numbers), then tuned page by page.

## The corrections that shaped it (the real story)
Most of the value came from being wrong first and fixing it. The first build used the template's default green and pitched at KMS, not Indicia. The brand was wrong twice before the v1.2 guidelines surfaced. The tone needed several passes. The lesson that kept repeating: **the brief is something you produce by shaping, not something handed over.** Audience, brand, and voice were all assumptions until corrected, and each wrong assumption cost a rebuild.

## Technical learnings worth keeping
- **Native header/footer beats fixed-position.** Playwright's `displayHeaderFooter` reserves the page margin and never overlaps content. A `position:fixed` header in Chrome's CLI print overlaps the body on continuation pages.
- **Pagination: break per topic, flow within, keep blocks whole.** This beats forcing one panel per page, which leaves sparse and cramped pages in equal measure.
- **Wide diagrams cannot bleed inline.** Bursting an element wider than its column collapses Chrome's print pagination (both the transform and the negative-margin methods, 25 pages down to 16). The reliable patterns are a fit-to-column image or a dedicated full-width page.
- **Logos stretch in flex columns.** A `width:auto` image in a flex column gets stretched by the default `align-items:stretch`. Height-only plus `align-self:flex-start` fixes it. Extract logos crisp from the guidelines PDF, not the low-res asset.
- **Don't keep patching one print-ready file.** Repeated edits corrupt its CSS. Rebuild from clean source each time.
- **DOCX and Google Docs are low fidelity for this.** The web layout does not survive, and the brand styling is generated by Tailwind at runtime so converters see nothing. The PDF stays the master.
